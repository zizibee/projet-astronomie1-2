Common Ceramic Terms

There are many terms used in ceramics. Here are some examples:

Term: Definition: Example:
plasticity The ability of a material (clay) to be molded. Earthenware clay has a high plasticity; it is easy to work with.
slip Clay mixed with water to be more liquid. Used to attach clay together and to decorate. A bucket of slip:
pottery wheel
(also called a potter's wheel)		 A machine used to shape clay. It spins to make pieces symmetrical. A Shimpo pottery wheel:
throwing To shape clay on a pottery wheel. A man throwing a vase:
kiln An oven for baking and drying ceramic pieces. Electric kilns:
firing To bake ceramics in a kiln. Pieces about to be fired:
greenware Clay that has not been fired. A vase before firing:
leather hard A stage of greenware clay that is easier to work with as it has been somewhat dried; it is no longer wet clay. A leather hard piece:
bone dry A stage of greenware clay that has been drying so it can be fired. Bone dry pitchers:
bisqueware Clay that has been fired once, but not glazed. A pitcher after being bisque fired:
glaze A substance made of minerals applied to ceramic pieces for a glassy finish, an imperviability to liquids, and to make them safe to eat food out of. Glazes change colour after being fired. A bowl after and before glazing:
